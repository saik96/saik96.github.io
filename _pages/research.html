Insights from a pseudospectral study of a potentially singular solution of the three-dimensional axisymmetric incompressible Euler equation

Published with Puneet Sharma and Rahul Pandit.

We develop a Fourier-Chebyshev pseudospectral direct numerical simulation (DNS) to examine a potentially singular solution of the radially bounded, three-dimensional (3D), axisymmetric Euler equations [G. Luo and T.Y. Hou, Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA 111, 12968 (2014)]. We demonstrate that (a) the time of singularity is preceded, in any spectrally truncated DNS, by the formation of oscillatory structures called tygers, first investigated in the one-dimensional (1D) Burgers and two-dimensional (2D) Euler equations; (b) the analyticity-strip method can be generalized to obtain an estimate for the (potential) singularity time.

Publication
Sai Swetha Kolluru Venkata, Puneet Sharma, and Rahul Pandit. “Insights from a pseudospectral study of a potentially singular solution of the three-dimensional axisymmetric incompressible Euler equation.” Physical Review E 105.6 (2022): 065107.
https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevE.105.065107

Novel spectral methods for shock capturing and the removal of tygers in computational fluid dynamics

Published with Nicolas Besse and Rahul Pandit.

Spectral methods yield numerical solutions of the Galerkin-truncated versions of nonlinear partial dif- ferential equations (PDEs) involved especially in fluid dynamics. In the presence of discontinuities, such as shocks, spectral approximations develop Gibbs oscillations near the discontinuity. This causes the numerical solution to deviate quickly from the true solution. For spectral approximations of the inviscid Burgers equation in one-dimension (1D), nonlinear wave resonances lead to the formation of localised oscillatory structures called tygers in well-resolved areas of the flow, far from the shock. Recently, the author of Besse(2024, submitted) has proposed novel spectral relaxation (SR) and spectral purging (SP) schemes for the removal of tygers and Gibbs oscillations in spectral approximations of nonlinear conservation laws.

For the 1D inviscid Burgers equation, it is shown in Besse(2024, submitted) that the novel SR and SP approximations of the solution converge strongly in L 2 norm to the entropic weak solution, under an appropriate choice of kernels and related parameters. In this work, we carry out a detailed numerical investigation of SR and SP schemes when applied to the 1D inviscid Burgers equation and report the efficiency of shock capture and the removal of tygers. We then extend our study to systems of nonlinear hyperbolic conservation laws — such as the 2x2 system of the shallow water equations and the standard 3x3 system of 1D compressible Euler equations. For the latter, we generalise the implementation of SR methods to non-periodic problems using Chebyshev polynomials.

We then turn to singular flow in the 1D wall approximation of the 3D-axisymmetric wall-bounded incompressible Euler equation [G. Luo and T.Y. Hou, Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA 111, 12968 (2014)]. Here, in order to determine the blowup time of the solution, we compare the decay of the width of the analyticity strip, obtained from the pure pseudospectral method (PPS), with the improved estimate obtained using the novel spectral relaxation scheme.

Publication:
Sai Swetha Kolluru Venkata, Nicolas Besse, and Rahul Pandit. “Novel spectral methods for shock capturing and the removal of tygers in computational fluid dynamics ”, Journal of Computational Physics 519 (2024): 113446.
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jcp.2024.113446

Early time resonances in the three-dimensional axisymmetric Euler equations and their one-dimensional wall approximation

Published with Rahul Pandit

We investigate the potentially singular solutions of the three-dimensional(3D) axisymmetric Euler equa- tions(see Luo et al.(2014)) using asymptotic time-Taylor series methods for the velocity and vorticity fields. We find the analogue of the “eye of the tyger” reported in the 1D inviscid Burgers equations by Rampf et al.(2022) and discuss them here. The singularities that lead to the time- counterparts of the “tygers” that we report in our earlier pseudospectral studies of these models, are arranged in the complex temporal domain in the shape of an eye centered about the origin for the potentially singu- lar initial conditions. We also study the case of the simplified 1D Hilbert model that can be used to approximate the axisymmetric Euler flow at the wall.

Publication:
Sai Swetha Kolluru Venkata and Rahul Pandit. “Early-time resonances in the three-dimensional wall-bounded axisymmetric Euler and related equations.” Physics of Fluids 36.9 (2024).
https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0222257

Ideal incompressible axisymmetric MHD: Uncovering finite-time singularities

Submitted with Rahul Pandit

We investigate the 3D incompressible ideal magnetohydrodynamic(IMHD) equations for the develop- ment of potential singularities, using the background velocity generated by the initial condition of Luo et al.(2014) to generate a potentially singular velocity field. In particular, we employ our spectral DNS to carry out analogous measurements of the width of the analyticity strip, which is now defined for the magnetic and kinetic fields, respectively as discussed in the work of Brachet et al.(2013).

Submission:
Sai Swetha Kolluru Venkata and Rahul Pandit. “Ideal incompressible axisymmetric MHD: Uncovering finite-time singularities” arXiV (coming soon).

Lagrangian studies of singularities in the 1D Hou-Luo model

Ongoing with Cornelius Rampf

We construct a Lagrangian theory for the 1D Hou–Luo (HL) model which is the wall-approximation of the 3D-axisymmetric Euler equation. We derive the Lagrangian time-Taylor coefficients using recursion relations and perform asymptotic analysis of the coefficients to deduce the locations of the singularities. We find that we are able to extend the Eulerian results of Kolluru et al. (2024) and that the singularities are arranged in the shape of an eye about the origin in the complex plane. Using the Lagrangian theory, we can find the time at which the finite-time singularity occurs for this 1D model.
